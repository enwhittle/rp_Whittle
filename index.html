Comparison of U.S. Wind Power and Fossil Fuel Energy Capacity and Land Usage

Elizabeth Whittle

Background: Wind Power

Renewable energy and wind energy generation, usage, and viability are increasing.

  • According to the US Energy Information Administration (EIA), utility-scale renewable energy sources generated 21.4% of the total energy generation in the US in 2023.
  • According to the EIA, in 2023, wind power made up the largest portion (47.5%) of US utility-scale renewable energy generation.
  • Engineering advancements, like increasing the hub heights and rotor diameters of wind turbines, have increased its energy yield and efficiency.

Background: Fossil Fuels

However, fossil fuels are still dominant.

  • In 2023, fossil fuels accounted for about 60.0% of energy generation in the US according to the EIA.

The viability of an energy source depends on many factors.

  • Cost, social acceptance, environmental and health impact, etc.

Rationale

Land use efficiency is an important factor for an energy sourceâ€™s viability. Finding how fossil fuels and wind power compare in terms of land efficiency can help environmental engineers identify and solve problems in the way of increasing wind power usage.

Objectives

  1. Find and compare land use efficiency (MW/ac) between energy sources
    • Onshore wind
    • Offshore wind
    • Fossil fuel power plants
  2. Find and compare land use efficiency (MW/ac) between regions of the US
    • West (CA, NV, OR, WA, ID, MT, WY, UT, CO, AK, HI)
    • Southwest (AZ, NM, TX, OK)
    • Midwest (ND, SD, NE, KS, MN, IA, MO, WI, IL, IN, MI, OH)
    • Northeast (ME, NH, VT, NY, PA, NJ, CT, RI, MA)
    • Southeast (AR, LA, MS, KY, TN, AL, WV, MD, VA, NC, SC, GA, FL, DE)
    • Territories (GU, PR)
